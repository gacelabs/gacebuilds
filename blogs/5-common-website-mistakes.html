5 Common Website Mistakes Filipino Small Businesses Make (And How to Avoid Them)

November 28, 2025 6 min read

Your website is often the first impression potential customers have of your business. Don't let these common errors hurt your credibility and lose you customers. Here's what to watch out for and how to fix them.

Mistake #1: No Mobile Optimization

Over 80% of Filipino internet users browse on mobile phones. If your website is hard to read or navigate on a smartphone, you're losing the majority of your potential customers.

Signs your site isn't mobile-friendly:

  • Text is too small to read without zooming
  • Buttons are too close together to tap accurately
  • You have to scroll horizontally to see content
  • Images appear broken or oversized
  • Navigation menu is difficult to use

The Solution: Always test your website on mobile devices. A responsive design automatically adjusts to any screen size. When building a new website, insist on a "mobile-first" approach where the mobile experience is designed before the desktop version.

Mistake #2: Missing Contact Information

Many business websites bury their contact information or don't include it at all. Customers who can't easily find how to reach you will simply go to a competitor.

Where contact info should appear:

  • In the header (phone number is most common)
  • In the footer on every page
  • On a dedicated Contact page
  • On the About page

The Solution: Put your phone number and email in the header. Have a dedicated Contact page with multiple ways to reach you – phone, email, messenger, and a contact form. Include your business hours so customers know when to expect a response.

Mistake #3: Slow Loading Speed

Filipino internet can be slow and expensive. If your website takes more than 3 seconds to load, over half of visitors will leave before seeing anything.

Common causes of slow websites:

  • Large, unoptimized images
  • Too many plugins or scripts
  • Poor quality hosting
  • No caching enabled
  • Too many fonts or external resources

The Solution: Optimize images before uploading (use tools like TinyPNG), minimize the use of heavy scripts, and choose a fast hosting provider. Test your speed at tools like Google PageSpeed Insights or GTmetrix.

Mistake #4: Outdated Information

Nothing says "abandoned business" like a website with last year's promotions or outdated business hours. Customers will question if you're even still operating.

"An outdated website is worse than no website at all – it actively damages your credibility."

Things to check regularly:

  • Business hours (especially holiday hours)
  • Pricing information
  • Promotions and special offers
  • Staff or team information
  • Contact details
  • Copyright year in the footer

The Solution: Review your website at least monthly. Set calendar reminders to update seasonal content. Remove expired promotions immediately. If you can't maintain it yourself, consider a maintenance plan with your web developer.

Mistake #5: No Clear Call to Action

Many websites describe services beautifully but never tell visitors what to do next. Don't leave customers guessing – guide them toward the action you want them to take.

Examples of clear calls to action:

  • "Call Now for a Free Quote"
  • "Book Your Appointment"
  • "Visit Our Store Today"
  • "Send Us a Message"
  • "Download Our Menu"
  • "Get Started"

The Solution: Every page should have a clear next step. Use buttons that stand out visually. Make the action obvious and easy. Don't overwhelm visitors with too many options – focus on one or two primary actions per page.

Quick Checklist for Your Website

Use this checklist to audit your current website:

  • ☐ Site loads quickly on mobile (under 3 seconds)
  • ☐ Easy to read and navigate on a smartphone
  • ☐ Contact information is visible and accessible
  • ☐ All information is current and accurate
  • ☐ Every page has a clear call to action
  • ☐ Images are clear and professional
  • ☐ No broken links or error pages
  • ☐ SSL certificate installed (https://)
