Getting Found on Google: Local SEO for Philippine Businesses

December 5, 2025 7 min read

When potential customers search for "bakery near me" or "electrician in Makati," will they find your business? Local SEO helps your business appear in these crucial searches. No technical background required – just follow these practical steps.

What is Local SEO?

Local SEO (Search Engine Optimization) helps your business appear when people search for things like "bakery near me" or "electrician in Makati." It's about being visible to customers in your area who are actively looking for what you offer.

When done right, your business can appear in:

  • Google Maps results
  • The "Local Pack" (the map and 3 business listings at the top of search)
  • Regular search results for local queries

Step 1: Claim Your Google Business Profile

This is the most important step. Go to business.google.com and claim your business listing. Fill out every field completely.

Key information to include:

  • Accurate business name (exactly as it appears on your signage)
  • Complete address
  • Phone number
  • Business hours (including holiday hours)
  • Business category (choose the most specific one)
  • Services or products offered
  • High-quality photos of your business

"Businesses with complete Google Business Profiles are 2.7x more likely to be considered reputable."

Step 2: Get Reviews

Ask happy customers to leave Google reviews. More positive reviews = higher rankings. Make it easy by sending them a direct link to your review page.

Tips for getting more reviews:

  • Ask immediately after a positive interaction
  • Send a follow-up text or email with a direct link
  • Include a QR code to your review page at your location
  • Respond to all reviews – positive and negative

Step 3: Use Local Keywords

On your website, mention your location naturally. Instead of just "web development services," write "web development services in Quezon City" or "serving small businesses across Metro Manila."

Where to include local keywords:

  • Page titles and meta descriptions
  • Headings (H1, H2, H3)
  • Throughout your content naturally
  • Image alt text
  • Footer (your address and service areas)

Step 4: Be Consistent (NAP)

Your business Name, Address, and Phone number (NAP) should be exactly the same everywhere – your website, Facebook page, Google listing, and any online directories.

Even small differences like "St." vs "Street" or "QC" vs "Quezon City" can confuse search engines and hurt your rankings.

Step 5: Create Local Content

Write about topics relevant to your local audience. This helps establish your business as a local authority.

Local content ideas:

  • Guides specific to your area ("Best places to eat in [City]")
  • Local event coverage
  • Customer stories from local businesses
  • Local industry news and updates

Step 6: Build Local Citations

Get your business listed in local directories and websites. In the Philippines, consider:

  • Yellow Pages Philippines
  • iYellow.ph
  • Yelp Philippines
  • Industry-specific directories
  • Local chamber of commerce websites

Results You Can Expect

With consistent effort on local SEO, you can expect:

  • Higher rankings in local search results
  • More organic website traffic
  • Increased visibility on Google Maps
  • More phone calls and inquiries from potential customers
  • Better return on investment compared to traditional advertising
