How to Choose the Right Domain Name for Your Business

November 10, 2025 5 min read

Your domain name is your online address – it's how customers will find and remember you. Choosing the right one is more important than you might think. Here's how to pick the perfect domain name for your Filipino business.

Why Your Domain Name Matters

Your domain name affects how customers perceive your business online. A good domain name is:

  • Easy to remember
  • Easy to spell
  • Professional sounding
  • Relevant to your business

7 Tips for Choosing a Great Domain Name

1. Keep It Short and Simple

Shorter domain names are easier to type and remember. Aim for 2-3 words maximum. Avoid complex spellings that customers might get wrong.

2. Make It Easy to Spell

Avoid words that are commonly misspelled. When you tell someone your domain name verbally, they should be able to type it correctly without asking for clarification.

3. Use Keywords When Possible

If it makes sense, include a keyword related to your business. For example, "ManilaWebDesign.com" clearly tells visitors what you do.

4. Avoid Numbers and Hyphens

Numbers and hyphens create confusion. People won't know if it's "5" or "five," and hyphens are easy to forget when typing.

5. Choose the Right Extension

For Filipino businesses:

  • .com - Most trusted, works for any business
  • .ph - Shows you're a Philippine business
  • .com.ph - Commercial Philippine business

6. Check Social Media Availability

Before finalizing, check if the same name is available on Facebook, Instagram, and other platforms you plan to use.

7. Think Long-Term

Choose a name that will still work as your business grows. Avoid being too specific or location-based unless that's central to your business.

Domain Name Ideas by Business Type

  • Service businesses: [YourName]Services.com, [Location][Service].com
  • Shops/Stores: [BrandName]Shop.ph, [ProductType]PH.com
  • Professionals: [YourName].com, [YourName][Profession].com

Where to Register Your Domain

Popular domain registrars in the Philippines:

  • GoDaddy
  • Namecheap
  • dot.ph (for .ph domains)
  • Google Domains

Expect to pay ₱500-1,500 per year for a .com domain.
